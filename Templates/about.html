About Organic Produce
        
Why Organic?
        

              Pesticides used by organic farmers are naturally developed, which are said to be less toxic.

              Health risks can arise from consuming too much food that has been contaminated with pesticides. For example, the commonly used pesticide roundup has been linked to 
                     delays in the development of infants.

              Fresh, organic foods contain more of vitamin C and A and fiber than packed foods.

              Cattle raised with conventional farming methods have an unusual diet, which can lead to an increased risk of heart disease if they are consumed.

              Eating organic can reduce your risk of your health being comprimised. Especially if you are pregnant, elderly, or suffering from allergies.

          
             
            
           
            
        

      

        
Why Shop Local?
            

                  Shopping Local keeps money within the local economy. Fewer or no payments will be made to larger distributors and corporations.

                  In order to make the long journey from a farm to a dinner table, crops are picked while they are still unripe, and thus need to be manipulated with chemicals once they 
                         arrive into the local ecosystem.

                   Keeping food production local ensures that the food will be ripe throughout its entire journey.
